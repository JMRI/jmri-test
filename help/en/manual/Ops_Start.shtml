Using DecoderPro® to Operate Trains

Starting DecoderPro®

Besides programming your decoders, DecoderPro® can be used to operate your railroad on DCC using your computer ( laptop or notebook is very convenient) and your DCC system. It can replace the throttle controls without having to use all the knobs and buttons. The number of locomotives that you may operate is limited only by your layout and your computer screen will display area. The Scenerio used for operating in this manual is A Digitrax Chief with DecoderPro V2.4 installed on a Windows XP platform. Other systems will vary, but should be basically similar in operating and requirements. We will assume a simple layout with one block, either manually operated or accessory power operated switches.

Getting Started

Connect your Hardware

Connect your computer to your DCC system via the serial bus (USB adapter or Serial connector) to your communications link, in the case of Digitrax, that would be to the LocoNet via either MS100, LocoBuffer, or PR3. Other systems have their own communications links.

Make sure your booster is connected to your layout using "track" power output and operating.

Starting DecoderPro®

Start DecoderPro program and the opening Screen will be displayed along with any windows or panes that were set to open in preferences. At a minimum you will want to open a throttle to operate a locomotive.

The throttle may be displayed on your computer screen by having set a throttle to open in your preferences, or in preferences set a throttle button to display, or open a throttle from the Tool menu.

Some systems allow you to control the track power on/off. In that case you may want to open the Power Control tool from the Tool menu.

If you need to monitor commands to your DCC system, you may open the Monitor Communications Window from the system dependent menu (in this case LocoNet menu).

Now we are ready to run trains.

Running a locomotive

Now select your locomotive of choice and place it on the track.
